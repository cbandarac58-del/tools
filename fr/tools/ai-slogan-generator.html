Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a slogan and a tagline?

A tagline represents your business as a whole and rarely changes (e.g., 'Just Do It'). A slogan is often created for specific marketing campaigns, products, or temporary promotions, though the terms are often used interchangeably in general marketing.

How do you write a memorable brand slogan?

Keep it under 6-8 words. Use strong verbs, focus on the primary benefit your product provides, and ensure it rolls off the tongue. Incorporating rhythm, rhyme, or a clever play on words drastically improves memorability.

Why do businesses need catchphrases?

Catchphrases and slogans act as cognitive shortcuts for consumers. When executed well, they instantly communicate your brand's core value proposition and emotional appeal without requiring the customer to read a lengthy 'About Us' page.

Can I use these slogans for marketing campaigns?

Absolutely! The slogans generated by this tool are free to use. However, before heavily investing in a specific slogan for a major campaign, it is advisable to do a quick trademark search to ensure a competitor isn't already using it.

What makes a slogan stick in people's minds?

Slogans stick when they trigger an emotional response (joy, security, motivation) and utilize literary devices. Repetition, alliteration (e.g., 'Bed Bath & Beyond'), and rhythm make the phrase feel like a song lyric, cementing it in long-term memory.
