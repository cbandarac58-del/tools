About the Volume Converter

This volume converter allows you to instantly convert between metric, imperial, and US customary units of volume. Volume measures the three-dimensional space occupied by a substance, typically liquids or gases, and is essential for cooking, science, and everyday fluid measurements.

Understanding the difference between US and Imperial measurements is crucial, especially in cooking and fuel economy. A key distinction is that 1 US gallon is exactly 3.78541 liters, whereas 1 Imperial gallon is 4.54609 liters. Other standard conversions include 1 liter equaling 1000 milliliters (mL), and 1 US cup equaling roughly 236.588 mL.

Common use cases involve baking recipes sourced from international websites, converting fuel prices from gallons to liters, or calculating chemical mixtures in a lab. Simply enter your value to get immediate, precise volume conversions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many liters are in a gallon?

One US liquid gallon is equal to exactly 3.78541 liters. However, if you are using the Imperial gallon (common in the UK), it is equal to approximately 4.54609 liters.

How do I convert milliliters to fluid ounces?

To convert milliliters to US fluid ounces, divide the number of milliliters by 29.5735. For Imperial fluid ounces, divide by 28.4131.

How many cups are in a liter?

There are about 4.22675 US cups in a liter. For metric cups (250 mL each), exactly 4 cups make a liter.

What is the difference between US and Imperial gallons?

A US gallon is defined as 231 cubic inches or exactly 3.785411784 liters, while an Imperial gallon is 4.54609 liters. The Imperial gallon is roughly 20% larger than the US gallon.

How do I convert cubic meters to liters?

Multiply the volume in cubic meters by 1,000 to get liters. For example, 1 cubic meter is equal to 1,000 liters.
